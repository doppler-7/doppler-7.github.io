software-defined receiver · listening log · last updated 2019-04-29

If you are reading this, the station is unattended. I left in a hurry and I did not take the dish down, so it is probably still pointed where I left it pointed. Do not assume that is an accident.

The candidate I logged repeats on a sidereal clock, which interference does not do. The back-bearing does not run to the star they named. It runs along a line, and a line is read from both ends. One end is a dead dish in Ohio. The other end is here:

8°20'14.06"N  80°23'23.46"E

Stand there. Dial it. That is the only instruction in this whole log that I am sure of.

I have to write down the other thing, because I have not been able to stop hearing it since the return, and writing it is the only way I have found to set it down.

It is a sentence. I did not compose it and I do not understand all of its words, and it is dated to a year I will not live to see. I am recording it exactly:

In 2037, on the 7th of Iris, on the Deimos colony, Centauri Plaza, log in: a-haax 0013.

A 2019 log should not contain a 2037 address. I know that. I am telling you anyway, because you are going to reach a point where you stop being sure which direction time is running, and when you do, I want you to remember that someone reached it before you and wrote it down.
